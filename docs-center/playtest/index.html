Playtest Checklist

Use these prompts to validate whether the current loop is clear, balanced enough, and tactically legible.

Smoke Test

  • Start a 2-player game and advance both players through at least three turns.
  • Export the Dev Match Recorder JSON after representative human-versus-AI races so action tempo, Prime Division use, turbulence, and resource growth can be compared.
  • Confirm the startup pulse loop visibly moves through 1 -> 4 -> 2 -> 1.
  • Confirm captures do not divide factors out of the pulse.
  • Confirm excess resonance is reduced at end of turn.
  • Confirm each Race Vector craft travels horizontally on the log2(1 + Harmony) scale and climbs vertically with distinct primes.
  • Confirm each translucent pulse vector points horizontally to the correct logarithmic distance and equal pulses align.
  • Assign one AI pilot and confirm it completes several turns without human input or illegal actions.
  • Confirm only the signed-in account cockpit and one selected rival query panel are visible.
  • In a 4-player race, select each rival craft and legend entry, confirming the query screen and attack target update.
  • Change race settings and confirm the live match remains unchanged until Start Race is pressed.
  • Start Foothill, Mountain, and Everest climbs and confirm the 10, 20, and 40 prime thresholds appear throughout the UI.
  • Use the compact previous, play/pause, and next music controls and confirm the title and full jukebox stay synchronized.
  • Accumulate many captured primes and legal attacks, confirming card and action scroll areas prevent layout resizing.

Action Scenarios

  • Use a forced jump and check that the cost is floor(log2(pulse)) before the jump.
  • Discard captured primes for both addition and subtraction, checking that each costs floor(log2(prime)).
  • Use Prime Division with exact and non-zero-remainder division, checking the quotient, sacrificed token, and resonance formula.
  • Repeat one prime several times and confirm turbulence rises by 1, then 2, then 3; verify its surcharge applies to every optional action.
  • End active and idle turns to confirm turbulence halves once or twice respectively.
  • Try an illegal subtraction that would reduce pulse below 1.
  • Attack using a shared prime, then confirm the same prime cannot initiate another attack that turn.
  • Create equal pulses and resolve Prime Theft, checking that the turn ends in win, loss, and tie outcomes.
  • Run a 4-player local race and confirm all three AI rivals act at a visible pace.
  • Complete a race and confirm the winner route and restrained craft glow remain visible on the Race Vector.

Questions

  • Do logarithmic forced-jump and prime-modification costs constrain repeated growth without stalling early turns?
  • Does prime-count-based resonance decay create useful pressure or too much slowdown?
  • Are exact pulse collisions common enough for Prime Theft to matter?
  • Should capture choice happen before or after resonance gain in future variants?
  • Do Sprint (1,000), Run (50,000), and Marathon (1,000,000) produce meaningfully different game lengths?
  • Can players identify who is leading from the Race Vector without reading every player panel?
  • Does the heuristic AI feel purposeful, overly aggressive, or too predictable?
  • Does the AI escape the midpoint pulse band by converting efficient divisions into multiple jumps without burning scarce unique primes?
  • Which observed heuristic behaviors should become Cadet, Navigator, Vanguard, and Champion difficulty profiles or tutorial lessons?

Record observations in future markdown notes or issue tracker entries as playtests accumulate.
