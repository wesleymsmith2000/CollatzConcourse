Playtest Checklist

Use these prompts to validate whether the current loop is clear, balanced enough, and tactically legible.

Smoke Test

  • Start a 2-player game and advance both players through at least three turns.
  • Confirm the startup pulse loop visibly moves through 1 -> 4 -> 2 -> 1.
  • Confirm captures do not divide factors out of the pulse.
  • Confirm excess resonance is reduced at end of turn.

Action Scenarios

  • Use a forced jump and check that the cost is floor(log2(pulse)) before the jump.
  • Discard captured primes for both addition and subtraction, checking that each costs floor(log2(prime)).
  • Try an illegal subtraction that would reduce pulse below 1.
  • Attack using a shared prime, then confirm the same prime cannot initiate another attack that turn.
  • Create equal pulses and resolve Prime Theft, checking that the turn ends in win, loss, and tie outcomes.

Questions

  • Do logarithmic forced-jump and prime-modification costs constrain repeated growth without stalling early turns?
  • Does prime-count-based resonance decay create useful pressure or too much slowdown?
  • Are exact pulse collisions common enough for Prime Theft to matter?
  • Should capture choice happen before or after resonance gain in future variants?
  • Do Sprint (1,000), Run (50,000), and Marathon (1,000,000) produce meaningfully different game lengths?

Record observations in future markdown notes or issue tracker entries as playtests accumulate.
